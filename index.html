Act One
I

The Search Side.

How we find the right account behind a police request, even when the data comes in messy.
Surface 1 of 2 · The search side
Meet the
Unified Query.
One Metabase query. Every identifier the police might give us — including the authorisation code alone. Ranked results with a Match Score on each.
The problem it solves
Police data comes with discrepancies — sometimes only a last 4, sometimes a name with typos, sometimes an amount and a date. We used to run three separate Metabase queries and reconcile the results manually. The Unified Query replaces all three with one — fuzzy on names, strict on hard identifiers, ranked on the rest.
How the Query thinks

Three kinds of inputs. One ranked list.

Each identifier is checked independently. Hard filters need exact match. Fuzzy filters tolerate typos. Scoring signals boost the rank. Match on any one — the account appears.

✨
Just shipped — Authorisation Code support.   Police can now give us nothing but an auth code, and the Query finds the account. Powered by joining our Adyen tables — every transaction's auth code is already there. Tested and live.
Match Score 100 = perfect · 80–99 = strong · 50–79 = judgment call · below 50 = unlikely
🎯 The Username field is the smartest part. Even if a customer changed half their username, the other half still matches. The Query notices partial similarity in both directions.
Act Two

The Workflow Engine.

Once we've found the account — how the Generator turns a paste into a regulated response in one click.
Surface 2 of 2 · The workflow engine
Meet the
Generator.
One long form. Six features. The half-day reduced to a single click.
The problem it solves
Even after finding the right account, the work wasn't done. Twenty-five fields to copy by hand from Retool, Metabase, and the EID image. Bilingual response to draft. A verdict to calculate. A Word doc to format from scratch. And a ClickUp task to update — which 90% of the time wasn't happening at all. The Generator owns all of it.
